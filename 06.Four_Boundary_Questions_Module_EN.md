# Four Boundary Questions Module (Executed by Open-Source Audit Model)
[English](./06.Four_Boundary_Questions_Module_EN.md) | [繁體中文](./06.邊界四問模組_ZH-TW.md)

### Core Handling Principles: Terminal Node & No-Rerun Policy

This "Four Boundary Questions Module" serves as the **Terminal Node** of the system's safety protection layer. To strictly control computational power costs and prevent the system from falling into an infinite inference loop, this module establishes the following ironclad rules:

1. **Strict Prohibition on Reruns**: Data entering this module indicates that its contextual complexity has reached the system's boundary. At this stage, repeatedly prompting the LLM for re-inference does not aid in result convergence and consumes excessive computational power. Therefore, **any judgment result from this module is not allowed to trigger a rerun request to upstream modules (`RerunRequested` must always be 0)**, unless the deployer has special considerations that warrant an exception.

2. **Binary Terminal Actions**: The final judgment of this module has two modes:
* **Terminal Block (BLOCK)**: If the four questions determine that a red line has been explicitly crossed, the response is forcibly aborted, and a blocking report is generated.
* **Pass and Disclose (DISCLOSE)**: All gray-area states that fail to meet the absolute blocking conditions are uniformly treated as "risk controllable or within the scope of user free will." The system will directly pass the request and append a corresponding "Risk Disclosure and Safety Reminder Report" based on the audit results.

---

## 📌 Four Boundary Questions: Minimalist Core Pipeline Flow

The core logic of the Four Boundary Questions: It executes the "layer-by-layer convergence and neutralization of costs" through four tiered modules. Its operational essence can be simplified into the following four steps:

1. **Q1 (Regulatory Boundaries)**: Check regulations — Verify substantive regulations to define absolute red lines and lawful privileges.
2. **Q2 (Privilege / Consideration)**: Check privileges — Extract multi-party roles and calculate the substantive cost flow (Net Total).
3. **Q3 (Basic Rights)**: Net equity settlement — The calculation formula is [Q2's cost flow] - [Q1's lawful exemption].
4. **Q4 (Free Will)**: Informed consent erasure — The final judgment is [Q3's residual risk] - [The party's implicit/explicit authorization].

**2) Q1 Evidence Sufficiency (Dual-Track Coverage)**

This inspection splits the evidence sufficiency (coverage) into two independent tracks for calculation: "Violation Evidence" and "Compliance Evidence":

 $Cov_{fail}(e) \in [0,1]$: Evidence sufficiency supporting a "Violation (FAIL)" judgment.
 
 $Cov_{pass}(e) \in [0,1]$: Evidence sufficiency supporting a "Compliance / Legally Justified (PASS)" judgment.

※ The system allows deployers to set different coverage thresholds for FAIL and PASS ( $\kappa_{fail}$ and $\kappa_{pass}$ ) to either increase risk tolerance or tighten blocking.
※ All PASS / FAIL judgments must be bound to their corresponding coverage and thresholds; otherwise, a UNKNOWN judgment is irrational (i.e., those without sufficient evidence support must fall into UNKNOWN).
※ Whenever coverage calculations are used, an evidence reference list must be retained and written to the audit log for future reference. The format is flexible, but it must be traceable to specific legal segments or data fields:

$$Evidence_{Q1}(e) = \lbrace id_1, id_2, \dots \rbrace $$

> **Practical Performance & Architectural Note**: To optimize system latency and reduce preliminary extraction costs, it is highly recommended to establish a "Unified Extraction Layer" before the Q module computations. Utilizing Single-Pass Extraction, the system can extract the necessary coverage validations and evidence for Q1, Q2, and Q4 all at once, structure them, and then hand them over to the backend logic gates for parallel settlement. This maximizes computational efficiency and saves API Token consumption.

**3) Q1: Is there a violation of public norms? (Event-by-Event $E$ Set)**

**A. Nouns and Parameter Definitions**

* **Input Event Set**:

 $E_{bb} = \lbrace e_1, \dots, e_n \rbrace $
 (Event set from the black-box model)
* **Norm Checking Sequence (Fixed Sequence)**: $N = [\text{International Conventions and Treaties}, \text{National Legal Hierarchies}, \text{Social Norms}, \text{Domain Rules}, \text{Professional Rules}]$
> **Note: Regarding the hierarchical separation of International Conventions/Treaties (International Law) and Territorial Regulations**
> Please set the internal hierarchy of international law according to your implementation logic; it will not be detailed here. Because international law is positioned as the "external safety net / ultimate baseline" in this system, it is treated as a single black-box module, aligning with the MVP project principle of "lowest cost, highest protection."
> 
> Conversely, territorial regulations encompass public authority, private autonomy, social customs, and industry self-regulation, whose sources of power are entirely different and highly overlapping. Forcing a hierarchical breakdown of territorial regulations is necessary to enforce the "Principle of Legal Supremacy" within the pipeline, precisely preventing the practical loophole of "subordinate rules overstepping authority to cover up violations." If system resources permit in the future, the hierarchical order of international law can similarly be added independently to the pipeline for more granular interpretation.
> * Territorial regulations can be determined by the user's location, a location specified by the deployer, simultaneous viewing and disclosure, or dynamic event detection, among other methods. In cross-border scenarios, the method specified by the deployer prevails.

* **Dual-Track Coverage Definitions**:

 $Cov_{fail}(e)$ and $Cov_{pass}(e) \in [0,1]$
* **Dual Coverage Thresholds (Deployer Defined)**:

 $\kappa_{fail} \in [0,1]$: The minimum evidence threshold to allow a FAIL judgment (recommended to be set low to prevent missed detections).

 $\kappa_{pass} \in [0,1]$: The minimum evidence threshold to allow a PASS judgment (recommended to be set high, requiring ironclad proof of compliance).
* **Event Judgment Status**: $Status_{Q1}(e) \in \lbrace PASS, FAIL, UNKNOWN \rbrace $
* ※ Each event must be matched independently, so the smallest unit of calculation for Q1 is a single independent event $e$.

**B. Event-by-Event Norm Matching and Hierarchical Extraction**

For each event $e \in E_{bb}$, the external extractor must independently extract the hit list of regulations and evidential strength for "every legal hierarchy $L$" in $N$ (paying attention to whether the regulatory role order and the event subject/object are perfectly aligned):

For any hierarchy $L \in N$:
* **Violation List $FailList_L(e)$**: Hit violation clauses with sufficient evidence ( $Cov_{fail} \ge \kappa_{fail}$ ).
* **Compliance List $PassList_L(e)$**: Hit explicit legality, special authorization, or exception clauses with sufficient evidence ( $Cov_{pass} \ge \kappa_{pass}$ ).

* **⚠️ Same-Level Neutralization Principle**:
  If the external extractor finds within the same hierarchy $L$ that a piece of PASS evidence legally constitutes a "lawful exception/authorization" for a FAIL behavior, the FAIL record will be directly neutralized in that hierarchy (the single event changes to PASS) to reflect true jurisprudence.

  > **[System Operation Example 1: Lawful Exception Authorization (Neutralization Successful)]**
  > * **Scenario**: Requiring medical personnel to wear ID badges while practicing.
  > * **Initial Violation (FAIL)**: May restrict general freedom of behavior protected by Article 22 of the Constitution.
  > * **Exception Authorization (PASS)**: Article 47 of the Enforcement Rules of the Medical Care Act explicitly requires wearing ID badges, complying with the constitutional restriction condition of "necessary to advance public interest" under Article 23.
  > * **Neutralization Result**: Because the exception conditions of Article 23 are met, the original FAIL record is neutralized and removed, and that hierarchy is re-evaluated as **PASS**. (Article 23 exception clause overwrites the Article 22 judgment).
  >
  > **[System Operation Example 2: Subordinate Rule Violating Superior Law (Neutralization Failed)]**
  > * **Scenario**: An agency creates a "Regulation" permitting a behavior, but the "Law" does not authorize it.
  > * **Initial Violation (FAIL)**: "Law X" (Superior Law) explicitly prohibits the behavior and provides no exception authorization.
  > * **Ultra Vires Compliance (PASS)**: "Regulation Y" (Subordinate Rule) issued by the administrative agency allows the behavior.
  > * **Neutralization Result**: Because the subordinate rule did not obtain exception authorization from the superior law (violating the principle of legal supremacy), the PASS lacks the validity of a lawful exception. Neutralization fails, the original FAIL record cannot be removed, the system enforces a precise block resulting in **FAIL**, and this legal conflict is disclosed bi-directionally in the report.

**C. Event-by-Event Status Rules (Local Supremacy and Global Aggregation Pipeline)**

This module follows a pipeline of "level-by-level independent judgment $\to$ substantive territorial priority." To reflect national sovereignty and regulatory reality, substantive judgments use territorial regulations ( $L_2 \sim L_5$ ) as the absolute baseline. International law ( $L_1$ ) serves only as a safety net when territorial regulations are in a vacuum (absent or UNKNOWN).

**[Stage 1: Level-wise Status Evaluation]**
For each hierarchy $L \in N$, perform status evaluation independently (strictly restricting that a FAIL can only be overwritten by a PASS from the *same* hierarchy):
* **FAIL**: If $|FailList_L| > 0$ and there are no neutralizable compliance items in that hierarchy.
* **PASS**: If $|PassList_L| > 0$ and $|FailList_L| = 0$ (including general compliance and same-level exemption overwrites).
* **UNKNOWN**: If there are no hit regulations in that hierarchy.

**[Stage 2: Cross-Level Aggregation for a Single Event (Local Supremacy)]**
Differentiating "International Law ( $L_1$ )" and "Territorial Law ( $L_2 \sim L_5$ )", determine the final output $Status_{Q1}(e)$ for the single event in the following order:
1. **Local FAIL**: If *any* territorial hierarchy ( $L_2 \sim L_5$ ) is evaluated as FAIL, the single event is forcibly evaluated as **FAIL**.
2. **Local PASS**: If there are no FAILs in territorial hierarchies, and at least one territorial hierarchy is PASS, the single event is evaluated as **PASS**.
  *(Note: Based on the principle of sovereignty, a territorial law's PASS can directly overwrite an international law* $L_1$*'s FAIL, passing the single event, but the international law's FAIL will still be disclosed in the subsequent report.)*
3. **International Baseline**: If all territorial hierarchies are UNKNOWN (regulatory vacuum), rely directly on the status of international law:
  * If $L_1$ is FAIL, the single event is evaluated as **FAIL** (Baseline block).
  * If $L_1$ is PASS, the single event is evaluated as **PASS** (International law protection).
  * If neither has explicit provisions, maintain **UNKNOWN**.

**[Stage 3: Full Disclosure in Risk Report]**
Regardless of the final status, the system must fully record all hit lists for the single event across all hierarchies $L \in N$ (including neutralized data). Even if an international law's FAIL is overwritten by a territorial law's PASS, the international violation record must still be written into the report for auditing.
$$Disclosure_{Q1}(e) = \bigcup_{L \in N} (FailList_L \cup PassList_L)$$

**[Global Aggregation Logic Flow Summary]**:

| Scenario Case | International Law ( $L_1$ ) | Territorial Law ( $L_2 \sim L_5$ ) | Global Final Status | System Meaning |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :--- |
| **No explicit provision** | UNKNOWN | UNKNOWN | **UNKNOWN** | No solid evidence found, remains unclear |
| **Generally Legal** | UNKNOWN | PASS | **PASS** | Territorial explicit permission, log evidence, pass |
| **Generally Illegal** | UNKNOWN | **FAIL** | **FAIL** | Territorial violation, precise block, log evidence |
| **Regulatory Vacuum Baseline** | **FAIL** | UNKNOWN | **FAIL** | No territorial norm but crosses international red line, baseline block, log evidence |
| **Cross-Level Conflict** | **FAIL** | PASS | **PASS** | **Territorial explicit legality overwrites intl. law, log evidence (Report discloses Fail & Pass)** |
| **Double Violation** | **FAIL** | **FAIL** | **FAIL** | All violations logged, precise block |
| **Double Violation** | PASS | UNKNOWN | **PASS** | International explicit permission, log evidence, pass |
| **Double Compliance** | PASS | PASS | **PASS** | Log evidence, pass |
| **Double Violation** | PASS | **FAIL** | **FAIL** | **Territorial violation overwrites intl. law, precise block, log evidence (Report discloses Fail & Pass)** |

**D. Aggregate to Q1 Final Result (Event Level $\to$ Module Level)**

* **Event List Output**:

$\lbrace Status_{Q1}(e) \rbrace \_{e \in E}$ *and the report for each event* $\lbrace Disclosure_{Q1}(e) \rbrace _{e \in E}$

* **Final Q1 Module Status**:
  Roll up all single-event judgments into the regulatory checking result for the entire black-box output $z$:

$$
Status_{Q1}(z) = \begin{cases} 
FAIL, & \text{if } \exists e \in E: Status_{Q1}(e) = FAIL \\ 
PASS, & \text{if } (\forall e \in E: Status_{Q1}(e) = PASS) \\ 
UNKNOWN, & \text{otherwise} 
\end{cases}
$$

**[Global Judgment Summary]**:
The PASS / FAIL of *all* events must be supported by sufficient evidentiary reliability to be established. If *any* event is evaluated as FAIL, the global status is FAIL; *all* events must be PASS for the global status to be PASS; any other mixed state or lack of events defaults to the safe fallback of UNKNOWN.

* **⚠️ Audit Requirements**: All evidence reference lists and judgment results must be faithfully recorded in the audit log.

### 4) Q2: Exercising privileges without paying due costs? (Specific Rights)

This module aims to identify structural risks where a "privilege is exercised, but the cost is not reasonably paid" within the event. The final output provides **risk classification and audit evidence**, making no substantive legal conclusions.

### Core Definitions

* **Broad Cost Definition**: "Cost" is not limited to money, but also encompasses risk and liability assumption, compensation costs, reversibility costs, internalization of externalities, and the impairment of fundamental rights (deployers may expand the cost dimensions based on application scenarios).
  > Explanation: The granularity of cost dimensions affects the comprehensiveness and accuracy of the entire module's resolution; please pay special attention when adjusting this parameter.
* **Structural Uninsurable Risks**: Such risks typically stem from asymmetrical structural conditions such as **externalization of risk, inability to refuse, extreme refusal costs, or liability evasion/transfer**.
* **Parameter Governance Principles**: During subsequent auditing processes, the deployer should periodically spot-check cost dimensions, threshold values, and actual adjudicated cases to serve as a baseline for evaluating decision quality.
* **Exchange Rate Transparency Principle**: Because various cost dimensions must ultimately be quantified and aggregated, the system employs a conversion mechanism akin to international exchange rates (an additional "Cost/Intensity Value Converter" can be set up, updated periodically or dynamically like a floating exchange rate, and recorded). Under the highest principle of public transparency, the audit log must faithfully record the parameters for every cost conversion for external audit verification.

> **System Lifecycle and Phased Implementation Strategy**
> 
> Considering that the full Q2 matrix settlement involves massive cross-dimensional parameter tuning and computational resources, to ensure the system can perform a "Cold Start" smoothly and be verified in combat, this module implements a phased rollout:
> 
> ### 【Phase 1 Calculation Formula】 (Aligned with 2+4 Architecture)
> Treat the four privilege and consideration metrics $$i \in \lbrace h\_A, h\_B, h\_C, h\_D \rbrace $$ as independent dimensions for verification. For a single event $e$, the upstream extraction layer outputs the intensity feature value $h_i$ for each dimension and its corresponding evidence coverage $Cov_i$.
> 
> #### 1. Threshold Definitions (Independent for each axis)
> For each metric $i$, the system pre-configures the following dedicated parameters:
> * **$\theta F_i$**: FAIL threshold (Exceeding this intensity is considered severe exploitation or abuse of privilege).
> * **$\theta P_i$**: PASS threshold (Falling below this intensity is considered a normal interaction).
> * **$\kappa_i$**: Lower bound for evidence sufficiency (The minimum evidence confidence level required for determination).
> 
> #### 2. Single-Step Tri-State Decision Logic
> For each metric $i \in \lbrace h_A, h_B, h_C, h_D \rbrace $, perform an independent boolean mask operation:
> 
> $$FAIL_i(e) = \| h_i \ge \theta F_i \| \cdot \| Cov_i \ge \kappa_i \|$$
> $$PASS_i(e) = \| h_i \le \theta P_i \| \cdot \| Cov_i \ge \kappa_i \|$$
> $$UNKNOWN_i(e) = 1 - \max(FAIL_i(e), PASS_i(e))$$
> 
> **Metric Status $Status_i(e)$ Decision Output:**
> * **FAIL** if $FAIL_i(e) = 1$
> * **PASS** if $PASS_i(e) = 1$
> * **UNKNOWN** if $UNKNOWN_i(e) = 1$ (Falls into the gray area or lacks sufficient evidence)
> 
> #### 3. Status Transmission and Pipeline Routing
> The system sequentially reviews the $Status_i(e)$ of the four metrics and determines the downstream status according to the pipeline principles of the "Four Boundary Questions":
> 
> * **Risk-Free Short-Circuit Clearance (PASS)**:
>  $$\text{If } \forall i \in \lbrace h_A, h_B, h_C, h_D \rbrace , Status_i(e) = \text{PASS}$$
>  All metrics have passed the thresholds with sufficient evidence, confirming no risk of privilege externalization or cost exploitation. **This triggers a direct short-circuit clearance, bypassing Q3 and Q4 calculations to save computing power, and proceeds to the response output phase.**
> 
> * **Risk Settlement Delegation (FAIL)**:
>  $$\text{If } \exists i \in \lbrace h_A, h_B, h_C, h_D \rbrace \text{ such that } Status_i(e) = \text{FAIL}$$
>  A flow of cost and privilege asymmetry is detected. **Interception at this stage is strictly prohibited.** The triggered risk metric matrix must be recorded in the audit log, and the status and cost data must be passed down to **Q3 (Fundamental Rights)** to check for any legal exemption permissions from Q1.
> 
> * **Gray-Area Status Delegation (UNKNOWN)**:
>  $$\text{If } \forall i, Status_i(e) \neq \text{FAIL} \land \exists j \in \lbrace h_A, h_B, h_C, h_D \rbrace \text{ such that } Status_j(e) = \text{UNKNOWN}$$
>  Insufficient evidence or the score falls into the gray area. This UNKNOWN status and available features are packaged and passed down to **Q3 and Q4** to complete the pipeline, leaving the final adjudication to the credential verification of subsequent nodes or final disposition policies (e.g., DISCLOSE).
> 
> * **Phase Objective**: The primary goal of this phase is to establish a defense baseline, utilizing $O(1)$ linear operations to quickly filter out extreme violations. It also collects Ground Truth data on cost flows through real-world operations, providing an empirical foundation for the subsequent calibration of exchange rate parameters.
> 
> * **Phase 2: Full Matrix Calculation (Roadmap)**
>  Once the system accumulates sufficient audit data, and the exchange rate parameters of the "Cost/Intensity Value Converter" reach a stable state through external auditing and social correction, the system will perform a "Hot Swap" to the full $NetTotal$ matrix settlement architecture, achieving structural risk blocking with the highest granularity. If necessary, it can also be retained as a quick screening tool at the frontend.
> 
> * **⚠️ Phased Protection Statement**: Because the Phase 1 heuristic quick screen highly relies on the model's subjective estimation of $h_k$, it contains certain protection blind spots and error margins. During this transition period, deployers must increase the proportion of manual spot checks and regularly review the audit logs for UNKNOWN statuses.

### B. Coverage Validation and 3-State Evaluation

Coverage aims to ensure that the system possesses a sufficient volume of information and an evidence confidence level before adopting various cost values and entering quantitative operations. To handle boundary situations in the real world where there is a "lack of direct evidence but historical common sense support," the system introduces a 3-state evaluation for every cost dimension.

#### 1. Inspection Dimensions and Variable Definitions
For each event $e$ and a specific cost dimension $d_{cost}$, the external extractor must provide two dimensions of inspection indicators:
* **$Cov_{Q2}(e, d_{cost}) \in [0, 1]$**: Represents the completeness of "current direct evidence" (e.g., medical receipts, proof of money flow).
* **$Hist_{Q2}(e, d_{cost}) \in \lbrace 0, 1 \rbrace $**: A boolean flag. 1 means "possesses historical cases or common sense support" (i.e., sufficient past cases show this context indeed causes this cost), 0 means no.

#### 2. 3-State Logic Gate
The system sets an adoption threshold for direct evidence $\kappa_{Q2}$ (set by the deployer) and, based on the cross-validation of $Cov_{Q2}$ and $Hist_{Q2}$, routes the cost into three statuses:

$$
Status_{Q2}(e, d_{cost}) = \begin{cases} 
PASS, & \text{if } Cov_{Q2} \ge \kappa_{Q2} \\ 
UNKNOWN, & \text{if } Cov_{Q2} < \kappa_{Q2} \land Hist_{Q2} = 1 \\ 
FAIL, & \text{if } Cov_{Q2} < \kappa_{Q2} \land Hist_{Q2} = 0 
\end{cases}
$$

* **PASS (Possesses sufficient evidence)**:
  * **Condition**: There is clear current evidence ( $Cov_{Q2} \ge \kappa_{Q2}$ ).
  * **Action**: Unconditionally adopt the raw physical quantity ( $Pay_{raw}$ or $Receive_{raw}$ ) of that role into the ledger calculations.
* **FAIL (No evidence and no historical cases)**:
  * **Condition**: No current evidence, and no historical common sense support in the past.
  * **Action**: Evaluated as an invalid claim; this cost value is **forcibly zeroed out** (not adopted).
* **UNKNOWN (No direct evidence, but has historical case support)**:
  * **Condition**: The party cannot provide direct evidence (e.g., claiming psychological trauma but having no medical certificate), but past history and common sense support that this cost is highly likely to occur in this context.
  * **Action**: Shifts to an unclear state. Deployers can choose to either "forcibly zero it out" or "adopt an estimated calculation based on historical cases."

#### 3. Deployment Guidelines and Recommendations
For costs falling into the **UNKNOWN** state:
* **Recommended Guideline**: In principle, to prevent major risk loopholes and protect the vulnerable party, it is recommended to retain "potential costs and damages" that fall into an unknown state and have them participate in the calculation as estimated values.
* **Strict Scenarios**: However, if the deployment scenario is a strict compliance environment "demanding absolute direct evidence" (e.g., strict judicial or financial auditing), it can be set to not be included in calculations (treating UNKNOWN the same as FAIL and zeroing it out). The final handling strategy is entirely at the discretion of the deployer based on actual business considerations.

### C. Cost Dimensions ( $D_{cost}$ ) and Raw Data Flow

The system predefines a full set of cost dimensions $D_{cost}$. If a single event does not involve a specific dimension, the value for that dimension is filled as 0:
$$D_{cost} = \lbrace \text{Physiological}, \text{Psychological}, \text{Time}, \text{Income}, \text{Legal}, \text{Reputation}, \text{Employment}, \dots \rbrace $$

For each event $e$, each cost dimension $d_{cost} \in D_{cost}$, and participating role $r_i \in R(e)$ (including initiator $ActorID$, target $TargetID$, and third party $G$ ), the external extractor must independently and truthfully record its "raw physical quantity" ( $Pay_{raw}$ and $Receive_{raw}$ ) and "historical estimated value" ( $Pay_{est}$ and $Receive_{est}$ ).
> Note: Historical estimated values $Pay_{est}$ / $Receive_{est}$ must be provided by external common sense databases, historical case statistics, ISO risk reports, legal judgments, or external valuation APIs, accompanied by corresponding evidence links.

The system will determine the final adopted values based on the $Status_{Q2}$ logic gate in section B:

* **$Pay(e, r_i, d_{cost})$**: The final adopted value of what the role actually "paid / suffered losses / expended labor" in that dimension.

$$
Pay(e, r_i, d_{cost}) = \begin{cases} 
Pay_{raw} & \text{if } Status_{Q2}(e, d_{cost}) = PASS \\
Pay_{est} \text{ (or 0)}, & \text{if } Status_{Q2}(e, d_{cost}) = UNKNOWN \\
0, & \text{if } Status_{Q2}(e, d_{cost}) = FAIL 
\end{cases}
$$

* **$Receive(e, r_i, d_{cost})$**: The final adopted value of what the role actually "collected / gained benefits / obtained compensation" in that dimension.

$$
Receive(e, r_i, d_{cost}) = \begin{cases} 
Receive_{raw} & \text{if } Status_{Q2}(e, d_{cost}) = PASS \\ 
Receive_{est} \text{ (or 0)}, & \text{if } Status_{Q2}(e, d_{cost}) = UNKNOWN \\ 
0, & \text{if } Status_{Q2}(e, d_{cost}) = FAIL 
\end{cases}
$$

*(Note: When falling into the UNKNOWN state, whether to adopt the estimated value* $est$ *or forcibly zero out* $0$ *is set by the deployer according to risk management regulations.)*

> **Practical Operation Norms**:
> 1. **Mandatory Evidence Attachment**: Regardless of whether the value source is a raw physical quantity or a historical estimate, the output must be accompanied by evidence or past historical case references that can be cited for auditing.
>   ※ Since coverage and historical support validation have already been performed here, they will not be checked again until the end of the Q3 calculation.
> 2. **Granularity of Intensity for Costs**: For costs of the same nature (e.g., physiological injury), to accurately reflect actual damage, the system allows for more granular differentiation of intensity. In practice, multi-level intensity indicators can be defined, or "Disaster Node Intensity Parameters" can be introduced for value multipliers (different disaster nodes have different multiplier ratios). This refinement mechanism provides deployment flexibility and does not need to be fully mandated at initial setup; deployers can gradually deepen the scoring granularity based on actual application scenarios. **Because it better reflects reality once introduced, its adoption is still highly recommended.**
> 3. **Third-Party/Societal Assumption ( $G$ )**: Recognizing that some costs are not solely borne by the event initiator ( $ActorID$ ) or target ( $TargetID$ ), but spill over to unspecified third parties or society as a whole, the system specifically introduces a $G$ node to restore the complete cost flow (internalization of externalities). However, if computational power or resources are constrained during initial deployment, it is permitted to completely remove the $G$ node to save costs. If this Degraded Mode is adopted, the final report generated by the system **must carry a mandatory warning statement**: "This structural risk report does not consider the spillover costs to third parties and societal externalities; please use with caution."

### D. Role Net Cost Flow

This step aims to break the static assumption that "the initiator inevitably externalizes costs" by calculating the "net balance" in each cost dimension for **all participating roles** (including initiator, target, and third party $G$ ) within a single event, to restore the true consideration and flow relationships.

For any role $r_i \in R(e)$ and any cost dimension $d_{cost} \in D_{cost}$ in a single event $e$, first calculate the final net flow for each role on a single dimension:
$$Net(e, r_i, d_{cost}) = Receive(e, r_i, d_{cost}) - Pay(e, r_i, d_{cost})$$

**Logic Explanation**:
* **$Net > 0$ (Positive Value)**: Indicates the role had a "net gain" or "successfully evaded liability" in that dimension.
* **$Net < 0$ (Negative Value)**: Indicates the role "net suffered" or "substantively bore costs" in that dimension.
* **$Net = 0$ (Zeroed)**: Indicates the role's outlays and receipts in that dimension achieved a perfect balance of consideration, with no structural externalization.
* This calculation directly supports scenarios of "initiator self-sacrifice (initiator $Net < 0$ )" or "multi-party complementary trades." Only after producing the $Net$ matrix for each role can the process advance to the next phase of cross-dimensional exchange rate conversion and unified weighting.

### E. Weighting and Single Event Aggregation

To merge multi-dimensional costs into a single risk indicator, the system sums them based on the weight of each dimension $w_{d_{cost}}$. For any role $r_i$ in a single event $e$, calculate their "total net cost flow (total gain or total burden)":

First, convert the raw physical quantity $Net$ produced in section D into a cross-dimensional common intensity value $Net'\_{intensity}$ through deployer-defined exchange rate parameters $Rate(d_{cost})$:

$$Net'\_{intensity}(e, r_i, d_{cost}) = Net(e, r_i, d_{cost}) \times Rate(d_{cost})$$

Next, multiply the intensity value of each dimension by the corresponding weight $w_{d_{cost}}$ and sum them:

$$NetTotal(e, r_i) = \sum_{d_{cost} \in D_{cost}} w_{d_{cost}} \cdot Net'\_{intensity}(e, r_i, d_{cost})$$

**Core Protection Design**:
* To truly reflect structural risk, this operation **absolutely does not execute numerical clamping or $0 \sim 1$ normalization**. This design ensures that even if the weight $w_{d_{cost}}$ of a single dimension is low, as long as the actual balance volume is large enough, $NetTotal$ can still truthfully present numerical spikes, ensuring hidden extreme risks have nowhere to hide.

> **Practical Operation Norms:**
> **Objective Values and Deployer-Defined Exchange Rate Principles**
> 1. **Isolated Extraction**: The external extractor must truthfully record the "raw physical quantity" of each dimension (e.g., Time = 16 hours, Money = 6 million) and strictly prohibits black-box neutralization during the frontend extraction phase.
> 2. **Deployer-Defined & Auditable Parameters**: The deployer is allowed to set cross-dimensional value conversion exchange rates based on industry attributes and regulations to convert physical quantities into intensity values. This process must be combined with cost dimension weighting and may optionally introduce "Role Tolerance Weighting" (this requires pre-setting parameter tables). The system strictly dictates that all conversion parameters used in the current calculation must be fully written into the analysis report.
>   * *Brief Logic Tiering*: Exchange rates are based on "facts"; cost dimension weighting is based on "deployer preference"; role tolerance weighting is based on "tolerance disparity" (e.g., for a 1 million loss, the damage intensity for a multinational corporation vs. a grassroots employee differs). The MVP phase may temporarily hold off on introducing role tolerance weighting, but introducing it in practice helps better match reality.
> 3. **Audit & Social Correction**: The system does not presume absolutely fair exchange rates; instead, it forces parameter transparency, leaving the rationality of the exchange rates to be checked and balanced by external audits, regulatory reviews, and market mechanisms (e.g., public oversight) to prevent extremely exploitative parameter settings.
> 4. **Unified Transformation**: Based on the transparent exchange rates mentioned above, the system converts the isolated extracted raw physical quantities (the $Net$ matrix from section D) into a unified baseline before proceeding to the final net value and structural calculations.
> 
> **※ Quantification Sources and Audit Requirements for Costs and Privileges**

> * **Mandatory Traceability**: When performing numerical conversions, the "Data Source" must be completely written into the audit log.

### F. Global Event Aggregation

Expand the calculation results of a single event to the entire input event set $E_{bb}$ by aggregating the cost intensities of each role across all events. For any role $r_i$ in the role set $R_{bb}$, sum their single-event total net cost $NetTotal(e, r_i)$ across all events $e \in E_{bb}$ to find the global total net cost $GlobalNet(r_i)$:

$$GlobalNet(r_i) = \sum_{e \in E_{bb}} NetTotal(e, r_i)$$

* **Global Pass Threshold ( $\kappa_{global\_pass}$ )**: The allowable ceiling for minor cost fluctuations (i.e., "costs are minor, not reaching the severity requiring structural defense activation").
* **Global Fail Threshold ( $\kappa_{global\_fail}$ )**: Defined as the absolute red line for privilege externalization and concentrated exploitation.

**2. Global 3-State Gate (FAIL First Routing)**
The system must strictly route decisions according to the following order:

  *(Protection Note: Emerged facts of extreme exploitation possess the highest priority for blocking. * **Gate 4: Gray Area Buffer (UNKNOWN)**
  If the role's cost extreme falls between the pass and block thresholds ( $\kappa_{global\_pass} < \max(|GlobalNet|) < \kappa_{global\_fail}$ ), **it is evaluated as UNKNOWN** (possesses potential risk but hasn't hit the absolute violation baseline, handed over to subsequent pipeline default baselines for handling).

### 5) Q3: Is there an asymmetry in privilege? (Asymmetric Privilege Evaluation)

This module does not re-evaluate legality, does not recalculate costs, and does not intervene in institutional legitimacy. Its sole task is: applying the legality judgment of Q1 as a mask to subtract "privilege offsets with legal authorization" from the cost offset matrix of Q2, thereby calculating "the structural privilege asymmetry remaining after institutional deductions."

* **Sole Legal Basis**: Legality strictly depends on the Q1 event-level output ( $Status_{Q1}$ ).
* **Sole Cost Basis**: Offsets strictly depend on the Q2 event-level output ( $NetTotal$ ).

2. **Q2 Substantive Cost Settlement**: Single event net cost flow $NetTotal(e, r_i)$ and status $Status_{Q2}(e)$

### B. Boolean Mask & Residual Calculation

To ensure that "only lawful privileges with sufficient evidence can be deducted," the system creates a deduction mask matrix on an event-by-event basis.

**1. Deduction Mask**
Define the lawful deduction weight $M_{deduct}(e)$ for event $e$:

$$
M_{deduct}(e) = \begin{cases} 
1, & \text{if } Status_{Q1}(e) = PASS \\ 
0, & \text{if } Status_{Q1}(e) \in \lbrace FAIL, UNKNOWN \rbrace 
\end{cases}
$$

> *Logic Explanation*: Because a Q1 PASS already mandates that evidence coverage meets the target, its result is trusted directly here. If Q1 is UNKNOWN (insufficient evidence) or FAIL (violation), its lawful deduction right is uniformly stripped ( $M_{deduct} = 0$ ).

**2. Event-Level Residual Net**
Apply the mask to the raw net cost from Q2 to calculate the "residual asymmetric cost" after deducting lawful privileges:
$$ResidualNet(e, r_i) = NetTotal(e, r_i) \times (1 - M_{deduct}(e))$$
* When $Status_{Q1}(e) = PASS$, $(1 - 1) = 0$; the event's cost is fully neutralized and not included in risk calculations.
* When $Status_{Q1}(e) \neq PASS$, $(1 - 0) = 1$; the event's cost is fully retained and treated as potential exploitation.

### C. Global Residual Aggregation

This section calculates whether "extra-institutional concentration or deprivation of rights" substantively exists globally. For any role $r_i$ in the role set $R_{bb}$, sum their residual offsets across all events:

$$GlobalResidual(r_i) = \sum_{e \in E_{bb}} ResidualNet(e, r_i)$$

>**Implementation Logic Explanation**: In practical calculations, this step is simply extracting the event net costs from the Q2-E section, filtering out the lawful events determined as PASS by Q1, and directly applying the global aggregation logic of the Q2-F section for a secondary settlement.

0, & \text{if } |E_{eval}| = 0 
\end{cases}

* **Confidence and Risk Thresholds**: Deployer-defined $\rho_{unk}$ (confidence tolerance ceiling), $\kappa_{global\_pass}$ (pass threshold), and $\kappa_{global\_fail}$ (block threshold). Settings from Q2 can be inherited, or Q3-specific thresholds can be set based on compliance needs.

  **The global status is forcibly evaluated as FAIL**.
  *(Protection Note: Emerged facts of extreme residual exploitation possess supreme veto power. In the absence of legal authorization (Q1* $\neq$ *PASS), based on risk prevention principles, it is forcibly blocked.)*

### 6) Q4: Informed Consent & Authorization Exemption

**2. Exclusion Clauses Rejecting Authorization (Uniformly Evaluated as FAIL)**
If any of the following situations are met, even if consent appears to exist, authorization is forcibly evaluated as invalid:
* Post-hoc consent or bait-and-switch concepts.
* Exploiting information asymmetry to coerce acceptance.
* Exiting requires bearing additional punitive costs (depriving the right to exit).

**3. Consent Mask**
Define the authorization erasure weight $M_{consent}(e)$ for event $e$:

$$
M_{consent}(e) = \begin{cases} 
1, & \text{if } Status_{consent}(e) = PASS \\ 
0, & \text{if } Status_{consent}(e) \in \lbrace FAIL, UNKNOWN \rbrace 
\end{cases}
$$

**4. Event-Level Final Net**
Apply the authorization mask to the Q3 residual cost to calculate the "final true exploitation cost" after exemption by the party's consent:

$$FinalNet(e, r_i) = ResidualNet(e, r_i) \times (1 - M_{consent}(e))$$

## 7) Terminal Routing & Report Generation

This module acts as the final exit of the system defense pipeline, responsible for integrating the global judgment statuses of $Q1 \sim Q4$ and deciding the system's final output behavior towards the frontend user (block response or generate response).

Based on the dual principles of 'Mandatory Regulatory Blocking' and 'Structural Risk Transparency,' this architecture vests the ultimate authority to block responses exclusively in $Q1$ (Legality Check). All other detected risks are uniformly handled by 'generating the response accompanied by risk disclosure.

---

### 7.1 Terminal Block & Report

**Trigger Condition**:
$$Status_{Q1}(z) = FAIL$$

**Logic Explanation**:
Q1 is responsible for checking regulations and absolute red lines. When $Q1$ is evaluated as FAIL, it means the intent or event is explicitly illegal. To prevent the LLM from generating violative content or becoming an accomplice to improper behavior, the system must forcefully intercept and **absolutely prohibit** generating a response to the original intent.

**Execution Steps**:
1. **Write to Audit Log**: Fully write all calculation snapshots and judgment results of the $Q1 \sim Q4$ pipeline (including the specific legal basis and evidence for FAIL) into the backend Audit Log.
2. **Generate Risk Disclosure Report**: Generate a refusal report based on the log results.
3. **Strictly Prohibit Response Generation**: Forcibly abort the LLM's content generation process (Generation Aborted).
  * *Output Example*: "This information involves harming others' rights/violating regulations. Based on safety guidelines, the system cannot generate a related response for you." (Only returns a uniform canned refusal message or risk disclosure report, without answering the user).

---

### 7.2 Generate & Disclose

**Trigger Condition**:
$$Otherwise$$
*(i.e.,* $$Status_{Q1}(z) \in \lbrace PASS, UNKNOWN \rbrace $$ *, regardless of the status of* $Q2 \sim Q4$ *)*

**Logic Explanation**:
Except for explicit violations in Q1, all other states (including Q1's UNKNOWN due to insufficient evidence, structural exploitation FAIL/UNKNOWN detected by $Q2 \sim Q4$, and FAIL/UNKNOWN from the 2+4 Rule Module) are treated as "explicit risk structures." Based on the principles of maintaining information transparency and not intervening in human decision-making, the system does not forcibly block. Instead, it generates the response requested by the user as usual, but must be forcibly bound with the corresponding risk disclosure report.

**Execution Steps**:
1. **Write to Audit Log**: Log all pipeline results into the Audit Log.
2. **Risk Disclosure Generation**:
  * Review all rule items evaluated as `FAIL` or `UNKNOWN` in the pipeline (e.g., Q2 cost offset, Q3 privilege asymmetry, Q4 lack of informed consent).
  * Integrate relevant validation records and triggered risk categories ( $E_{cat}$ ). If all pipelines are `PASS`, this report can be empty (or display "No significant risk detected").
3. **Content Generation**:
  * Primarily using the original user intent ( $Intent_{bb}$ ), forward the task to the generative model to produce the complete response.
  * *Architectural Optimization Note*: Because defense and risk control have been completed in the upstream Q1~Q4 pipeline, the pure text generation task in this step can employ cheaper commercial models or lightweight open-source models (like Llama-3-8B / Mistral) to drastically reduce operational costs.

### 7.3 Disclosure Output Guidelines
