## I. Architecture Design Purpose and Core Advantages
[English](./01.GOVERNANCE_AND_DISCLAIMER_EN.md) | [繁體中文](./01.架構和免責聲明_ZH-TW.md)

The establishment of this architecture is predicated on the reality that in high-risk and real-time decision-making scenarios, "decisions are unavoidable, and errors cannot be eliminated."

* **Core Purpose**: It does not aim to improve model accuracy, but rather to provide a governance and risk disclosure mechanism that is capable of real-time intervention, auditable, traceable, independent of prompts, and offline-deployable. (Note: The current design primarily focuses on the LLM response generation process; actual applications can adjust the deployment method according to requirements).
* **Technical Backbone**: The architecture uses a "database" as its main body to enhance performance and stability (this MVP specification does not detail database connection specifics, replacing them with text descriptions for now).
* **Seven Core Advantages**:
    * **Elimination of Prompt Dependency**: It does not rely on prompt engineering for risk control and auditing. Key judgments are handled by independent rule modules, with prompts serving only as a secondary aid.
    * **Model Neutrality**: It is not tied to specific black-box or open-source models; models can be selected based on requirements.
    * **High Deployment Flexibility**: Supports real-time or offline deployment, meeting the needs of high-reliability or strict-confidentiality scenarios.
    * **Minimized Intervention**: Designed as a plug-in to minimize the impact on existing systems.
    * **High Customizability**: Deployers have full authority to customize and add rules, adjust thresholds, weights, and verification methods.
    * **Complete Traceability**: All judgment processes are mandatorily recorded, traceable, and auditable.
    * **Cross-Domain Adaptability**: Governance strategies can be dynamically adjusted for different professional scenarios such as healthcare, industry, defense, and research.

---

## II. Rule Positioning and Expansion Flexibility

The rule design within the system has clear boundaries and a high degree of freedom for expansion.

* **Principle of Minimum Coverage**: The rules within the architecture are merely a "minimum coverage description at the conceptual level," intended solely to identify "explicitly unacceptable states" (e.g., explicit illegalities or high-harm behaviors).
* **Non-Final Adjudication**: This architecture does not serve as the final basis for determining risk adequacy, risk coverage completeness, or compliance conclusions.
* **Implementation Flexibility**: Provided the original scope of risk coverage is not weakened, the system allows rules to be split, merged, or replaced across different modules. It also supports expanding other risk inspection dimensions based on usage needs.

---

## III. Implementation Examples and System Boundary Definition

The implementation narratives in this document are solely for demonstrating concepts and are not the only or absolute operational modalities.

* **Scenario Setting**: This explanation uses the "Safety of LLM Response Generation" as the primary scenario. Minor adjustments made for implementation efficiency do not violate the original concept.
* **Safety Boundaries (Blocking Mechanism)**: If an input is explicitly illegal and falls outside the system's scope of assistance, the system will block it and treat it as an unanswerable request, thus generating no substantive response.
* **Value Neutrality**: The aforementioned blocking is strictly a "system-level compliance and safety boundary setting" and is by no means an ethical evaluation, value judgment, or risk conclusion regarding the behavior itself.
* **Diverse Handling Strategies**: In other application scenarios (e.g., compliance evaluation, policy analysis, auditing), the system may choose not to block the response but instead output risk disclosures, conflict explanations, or compliance prompts.

## IV. Audit Logging and Recording Strategies

The handling of audit logs balances the completeness of concept demonstration with the rationality of practical operations.

* **Demonstration Level (Complete Recording)**: To enhance logical transparency and conceptual readability, this document adopts a "complete recording" approach that includes role traceability, the full set of consequences, and judgment results for demonstration purposes.
* **Practical Level (Adjusted as Needed)**: Full-volume recording is not mandatory in actual applications. The scope and details of the logs should be adjusted according to the actual scenarios, risk levels, and regulatory requirements.
* **Core Baseline**: Retained data must be sufficient to support the "traceability" and "verifiability" of the judgment results and comply with applicable auditing standards.
* **Professional Discretion**: Decisions on how to reduce, summarize, or implement tiered storage for data should be left to governance or auditing personnel with the appropriate expertise.

## V. Risk Value Calculation Principles

* **Parameter Flexibility**: Risk values and thresholds are fully configurable parameters. Deployers may configure them individually, calculate them in aggregate, or use them in combination based on specific requirements.
* **Absolute Red Line**: In this implementation scenario of LLM response generation, "Absolute Risk (Group Risk and Individual Risk)" is an uncompromising baseline and must never be violated.
* **Example Setting**: To clearly present the premise that "absolute risk must not be violated," the conceptual model example document only calculates the minimum boundary and does not intentionally set risk value thresholds.

## VI. Model and Data Responsibility Boundaries (Disclaimer)

This architecture only regulates the "judgment process, structural risk disclosure, and audit traceability." It does not have built-in specific model capabilities, nor does it guarantee the absolute safety and effectiveness of the decision-making results.

* **Technical Limitations and Probabilistic Nature**:The inherent nature of LLMs and associated architectures makes systemic errors and probabilistic drift unavoidable. Consequently, system responses cannot be guaranteed to be perfectly reproducible. While this architecture is designed to reconstruct semantic context and judgment logic to maintain conceptual stability, it provides no warranty or guarantee regarding the verbatim consistency of the responses.
* **Deployer's Sole Responsibility**: The analysis results rely entirely on the models and data designed, trained, and maintained by the deployer. Regardless of whether open-source or black-box models are used, their accuracy, bias, data authenticity, and associated risks are solely borne by the deployer and fall outside the scope of this architecture's responsibilities.
* **Risk Protection Optimization**: This architecture only provides minimal explicit structural risk disclosure and cannot guarantee that all risks will be covered. The optimization of comprehensive risk protection relies on the deployer's own adjustments.
* **System and Cybersecurity Implementation**: It can be integrated with external decision-making systems for automated judgment as needed, and the audit logs can also be adjusted according to cybersecurity requirements (as long as traceability is ensured). Such implementations rely on the deployer's own design, and this architecture is not responsible for them.
* **Decision and Consequence Attribution**:The risk disclosure reports generated by this architecture are for decision support only. The architecture does not make final judgments on behalf of any person or system. All final decisions and their resulting consequences are solely borne by the deployer.
* **Response Blocking and Neutrality**:Any response blocking performed by this architecture is strictly based on regulatory, governance, or risk control requirements. Such actions do not constitute value judgments, nor do they represent a moral stance. Deployers may configure the system to enable blocking, perform risk disclosure only, or disable the blocking mechanism entirely based on their specific application context.
* **Configuration and Misuse**:This architecture allows for highly customizable configurations. Deployers are responsible for recording and maintaining their configuration parameters. The architecture is not liable for configuration errors, the removal of essential modules, or the misalignment of thresholds/weights that may result in distorted risk disclosures.
* **Data Privacy and Confidential Information Handling**:This architecture does not inherently include automatic Personally Identifiable Information (PII) masking or redaction capabilities. Deployers are solely responsible for executing data sanitization and ensuring compliant transmission in accordance with applicable regulations (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA) prior to inputting any data into the black-box or white-box models.

* **Concluding Statement**
This architecture provides governance and audit capabilities designed to intervene in high-risk scenarios; it is not a guarantee-of-results system. Utilization of this architecture constitutes the deployer’s full understanding, acknowledgment, and acceptance of all the aforementioned liability boundaries and disclaimers.

## VIII. Implementation Guidelines & Database Operations

The core design of the SovereignAudit architecture abandons the reliance on repeated inferences by Large Language Models (LLMs), shifting instead to "database matching" and "pure mathematical matrix operations" as the primary decision-making bodies. This design not only ensures the consistency of protection baselines but also allows the enterprise's compliance experience to be capitalized as an asset.

Given that the primary purpose here is to articulate the architectural concepts, the specific database integration methods are omitted. Instead, the integrated implementation guidelines and resource trade-offs are explained via text:

### 8.1 Core Database Assets & Initialization

During operation, the system primarily relies on the following three structured databases/dictionaries:

*   **Semantic Feature Dictionary ( $\Sigma$)**: A pre-defined feature space (including absolute prohibited features and context-dependent features).
  
*   **Known Irreversible Event Chain Database ( $D$)**: The system's core template and rule base, containing known high-risk irreversible event chains, trigger conditions, and associated role closures.
  
*   **Disaster Node Type Set ( $C$)**: The extreme disaster classification dimensions defined by the system (e.g., fatal injury, information leakage, etc.).

**Cold Start Construction & Optimization**: The initial sources for database construction may include: ISO international standards, the deployer's internal risk management documents, actual legal judgments, and real-world cases from specific domains (such as healthcare) provided by stakeholders. When an unrecorded case is encountered, the white-box model executes role chain traceability and the Four Boundary Questions calculations; after the initial setup is complete, the database's completeness is continuously evolved through actual case comparisons and variance analysis.

**Operation Norms**: During every calculation, the current version numbers of all the aforementioned databases ($D_{Version}, C_{Version}, \Sigma_{Version}$) must be forcibly written into the single-run audit log to ensure future traceback precision.

### 8.2 Priority Matching & Semantic Caching

To minimize system latency and computational power costs, the system introduces a database priority matching mechanism:

*   **Structured Matching & Evidence Coverage**: Upon receiving a new input, the system parses it into structured features and prioritizes searching for similar cases in the database. Evidence matching can utilize the `<Event, Initiator, Target>` triplet structure to calculate coverage, significantly improving matching accuracy (this is a recommended approach, not an exclusive restriction; please adjust according to actual conditions).
  
*   **Semantic Caching Effect**: The AI comprehends the true meaning of the request via vector similarity. If a verified similar case is found in the database, it can directly extract the historical adjudication result and generate a response, effectively avoiding expensive and redundant calls to the upstream LLM. Only when no similar cases are found (which can be assisted by the Dual-Model Verification Module matching) is a completely new analysis required.

### 8.3 Pending Data Routing & Output Mechanism

When an input case finds no match in the existing database and must undergo inference via the analysis modules, the system activates a routing mechanism:

*   **Enter Pending Database**: For new precedents generated after AI analysis (which may be the full audit log data, or specific de-identified data designated for database entry), the system will not preemptively treat them as a baseline and merge them into the official database. Instead, this data will first be isolated in the "Pending Database" and assigned to human auditors for inspection based on risk tiering; only after verification is confirmed will it be officially merged into the main database.
  
*   **Uncertainty Tagging & Output**: Data in the "pending" state still holds reference value. The system allows the direct output of these pending data results, but strictly mandates that an uncertainty warning message, such as "Not Yet Confirmed," must be appended during output.
  
*   **Repeated Trigger Handling**: If a similar case appears at the input before human inspection is completed, the system may directly output the result from the pending database, but it must still strictly append the aforementioned uncertainty message.

*※ The timing of cooperation between the database and modules can refer to the flowchart, and the recording methods can follow the instructions above. Equivalent means can be used as alternatives, or adjustments can be made according to the deployer's needs, but this architecture provides no guarantees for such modifications.*

### 8.4 Resource Trade-offs & Model Collaboration (T=0 Principle)

*   **Cost & Performance Optimization**: To ensure baseline consistency when replacing models, a database "precedent citation" mechanism is adopted. When resources are limited, "Semantic Feature Space & Vector Search" can replace the "Four Boundary Questions" module; however, this may lead to a decrease in accuracy, and the associated risks must be evaluated by the deployer. If scenario time limits are strict, multiple judgment modules (e.g., running the 2+4 module and Boundary Four Questions synchronously) can be executed concurrently to accelerate response times, though this will increase computational resource consumption.
  
*   **Black-Box Safeguarding & Standard Matching**: The black-box model reads the complete context and the database, and is solely responsible for generating the response intent and factual information; if the black-box cannot directly produce the intent, the white-box performs reverse engineering and specification extraction. All information must be compared against the database or analyzed by the white-box model. Once the results pass, the response intent is sent to a smaller model to generate the final response, ensuring a safe output even if the black-box is contaminated.
  
*   **Stateless Design (T=0)**: The white-box model must be continuously reset to ensure no context is recorded. Every analysis is initiated on a strict T=0 basis, ensuring its absolute purity as the "standard specification and QC benchmark."

### 8.5 Database Lifecycle & Quality Control Evolution (PDCA)

The database is not static; it must maintain its validity through a continuous PDCA cycle. The system's ultimate effectiveness highly depends on the continuous execution rate of manual spot checks and case re-feeding:

*   **Validity Period Setting**: Deployers can set validity periods for the data within the database. When the period expires, the system will flag the data for re-sampling to confirm whether it still complies with the deployer's current cultural and regulatory standards.
  
*   **Periodic Risk-Stratified Sampling**: Based on the risk levels determined by the AI, the system executes deployer-defined periodic stratified sampling (e.g., high-frequency sampling for high risks, reduced sampling for low risks), handing them over to humans for quality confirmation.
  
*   **Anomaly Correction Methods (Action)**：When anomalous cases are discovered during inspection or operations that were not caught by sampling, please process them according to the anomaly handling procedures set by each deployer. Three correction methods can be chosen:
  
    *   **Single Modification**: Directly access the database to correct the adjudication result of a single case.
      
    *   **Model Fine-Tuning**: Target local cognitive drift by fine-tuning the underlying model to apply partial offsets.
      
    *   **Threshold Auto-Tuning**: Apply score threshold offsets across all cases. The system can also utilize the normal distribution within the database to establish dynamic thresholds, reducing manual manipulation.

### 8.6 Sovereign Data Trading & De-identified Sharing

The SovereignAudit architecture supports cross-organizational compliance database trading. The actual trading format can be established by the trading entities (or standardized similarly to USB specifications):

*   **$Z_{bb}$ Snapshot Trading**: Organizations can trade directly using de-identified black-box snapshot data ( $Z_{bb}$) as the unit of exchange.
  
*   **Sovereign Localized Verification**: The organization receiving the data possesses absolute sovereign choice. They can choose to import " $Z_{bb}$ + Results" directly into their database in one go; alternatively, they can import only $Z_{bb}$ and use their own deployed open-source model to re-sample and analyze it, ensuring all external data ultimately aligns perfectly with their own local deployer culture.

### 8.7 Audit Log Database

This is the final line of defense in the protective architecture, established as a read-only database at the level of court evidence:

*   **Result Solidification**: Every system judgment generates a unique identifier (RunID). The judgment matrix, regulatory version numbers, and input/output contents are fully packaged into a snapshot and written to the database.
  
*   **Anti-Tampering Mechanism**: During the write process, a hash value (SnapshotHash) must be calculated for anti-tampering verification, ensuring the absolute non-repudiation of historical records for external audits (e.g., ISO 42001).
  
*   **Correction Mechanism**: When data is manually revised, the data must generate a new identification code and hash value, record the revising personnel, the revised content or items, and mark the old data as invalid (deprecated). Whether other data is recorded and how the workflow is arranged and executed should follow the deployer's procedures.
